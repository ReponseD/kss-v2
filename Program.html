Our Programs

Academic Excellence at Kagarama Secondary School

Advanced Level (A' Level)

Kagarama Secondary School's Advanced Level program offers specialized education aligned with the Rwanda Education Board (REB) curriculum. Students choose from traditional science combinations and innovative national streams designed to prepare them for tertiary education and professional success.

Advanced Level (A' Level) Programs
About A' Level at KSS

The Advanced Level program at Kagarama Secondary School provides students with rigorous academic preparation aligned with the Rwanda Education Board (REB) national curriculum. Our A' Level offerings combine theoretical knowledge with practical application, preparing students for university admission and future careers in science, technology, and related fields.

Traditional Science Combinations

Kagarama Secondary School offers four traditional science combinations that have proven successful in preparing students for higher education:

PCM - Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics

Ideal for students pursuing engineering, physical sciences, and mathematical fields.

PCB - Physics, Chemistry, Biology

Perfect for aspiring medical professionals, biochemists, and health science students.

MCB - Mathematics, Chemistry, Biology

Designed for students interested in pharmacy, biotechnology, and life sciences.

MPG - Mathematics, Physics, Geography

Suitable for careers in environmental science, geophysics, and applied mathematics.

New National Mathematics & Science Streams

In accordance with REB curriculum reforms, Kagarama Secondary School has introduced innovative streams that broaden student opportunities and strengthen STEM competencies:

Stream 1: Mathematics + Sciences

Subjects: Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry, Biology

This comprehensive science stream provides students with a well-rounded foundation in all core scientific disciplines, enabling flexibility in choosing university programs and careers in diverse STEM fields.

Stream 2: Applied Sciences & Economics

Subjects: Mathematics, Physics, Geography, Economics

This innovative stream combines physical sciences with economic principles, preparing students for careers in environmental economics, urban planning, resource management, and analytical business fields.

Why These Streams Matter
  • REB Alignment: Fully compliant with Rwanda Education Board curriculum reforms
  • Expanded Options: More pathways for students to pursue their academic interests and career goals
  • STEM Excellence: Enhanced focus on Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics
  • Analytical Skills: Development of critical thinking, problem-solving, and research abilities
  • University Readiness: Comprehensive preparation for tertiary education admission requirements

Ordinary Level (O' Level)

The Ordinary Level program at Kagarama Secondary School establishes essential academic foundations through a comprehensive REB-approved curriculum. Students develop critical thinking, effective communication, academic discipline, and digital literacy skills necessary for Advanced Level success.

Ordinary Level (O' Level) Program
About O' Level at KSS

The Ordinary Level program serves as the critical foundation for students' academic journey at Kagarama Secondary School. Following the Rwanda Education Board curriculum, our O' Level program provides three years of comprehensive education that prepares students for Advanced Level studies and develops essential life skills.

Foundation for Academic Success

Our O' Level curriculum is designed to build strong academic foundations across all core subject areas. Students are exposed to a broad range of disciplines including languages, mathematics, sciences, humanities, and technology, allowing them to discover their strengths and interests before specializing at A' Level.

Skills Development

Beyond academic knowledge, O' Level students at KSS develop essential competencies that serve them throughout their educational journey and beyond:

  • Critical Thinking: Students learn to analyze information, question assumptions, and develop logical reasoning skills through problem-solving activities and discussions.
  • Effective Communication: Both written and oral communication skills are emphasized through presentations, essays, and collaborative projects in English, French, and Kinyarwanda.
  • Academic Discipline: Students develop strong study habits, time management skills, and personal responsibility that prepare them for higher education demands.
  • Digital Literacy: Modern ICT skills are integrated across subjects, ensuring students can navigate digital learning environments and utilize technology effectively.
Learning Environment

Kagarama Secondary School provides a supportive and engaging learning environment where O' Level students receive individual attention, mentorship from experienced teachers, and access to modern educational resources. Our teaching methods emphasize student participation, practical application, and continuous assessment to ensure mastery of essential concepts.

Beyond the Classroom

Co-Curricular & Non-Curricular Programs

Kagarama Secondary School offers enrichment programs that complement academic learning and develop practical skills essential for the 21st century.

Robotics Program

Our robotics program provides hands-on experience in STEM education through practical design, construction, and programming activities. Students participate in national and international robotics competitions, developing innovation, teamwork, and technical problem-solving skills.

  • Design and build functional robots
  • Learn programming and automation
  • Compete in regional and national competitions
  • Develop engineering and innovation skills

Mathematics Program

The mathematics enrichment program goes beyond classroom learning to challenge and inspire students with advanced problem-solving techniques. Participants engage in mathematical competitions and olympiads, building analytical thinking and mathematical reasoning abilities.

  • Advanced problem-solving strategies
  • Preparation for mathematics competitions
  • Olympiad training and participation
  • Peer learning and collaboration
